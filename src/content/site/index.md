# Home

## Welcome

This is the beginning of a shared workshop project rooted in practical work, stewardship, reciprocity, and learning together.

Right now, this is not a fully formed organization, business, nonprofit, or school.

It is simply:

* one person building a workshop,
* inviting others into the process,
* and experimenting with what becomes possible when people gather around meaningful shared work.

The goal is not rapid growth.
The goal is to create a durable place where:

* people can build useful things,
* young people can learn by doing,
* tools and knowledge can be shared,
* governance can be practiced collaboratively,
* and trust can grow slowly through participation.

This project starts small.

We are building from where we actually are — not pretending to already be something we are not.

---

## What We Are Building

Over time, this may become:

* a shared workshop,
* a tool commons,
* a learning environment,
* an apprenticeship ecosystem,
* a place for community projects,
* or a launch point for small local businesses.

But today:

We are mostly building the space itself.

That means:

* cleaning,
* organizing,
* repairing,
* experimenting,
* learning,
* helping each other,
* and slowly building the culture we want to exist.

---

## Core Principles

### Build Together

Shared work builds trust faster than discussion.

### Start Small

We value consistency and reality over ambition and hype.

### Learn By Doing

Participation matters more than credentials.

### Stewardship

We care for tools, materials, people, and the space itself.

### Reciprocity

People contribute where they can and receive support where needed.

### Participation Over Consumption

This is not a service people passively consume.
It is a place people help shape.

# Agreements

## Why Agreements Matter

The workshop depends on trust.

These agreements are not meant to create rigid control.
They exist to help us:

* work safely,
* reduce conflict,
* care for shared resources,
* and create an environment people want to return to.

Participation in the workshop means agreeing to help maintain the space and culture.

---

## Core Agreements

### 1. Leave Things Better Than You Found Them

Clean up after yourself.
Return tools.
Help maintain shared order.

The workshop only works if everyone participates in stewardship.

---

### 2. Ask Before Using Specialized Tools

Some tools require training, supervision, or coordination.

If you do not know how to safely use something:

Ask.

---

### 3. Respect People, Materials, and Time

We expect:

* honesty,
* direct communication,
* patience,
* and basic respect.

Conflict is normal.
Disrespect is not.

---

### 4. Participation Matters More Than Status

This space values:

* showing up,
* helping,
* learning,
* teaching,
* and contributing.

Not titles, credentials, or hierarchy.

---

### 5. Safety Comes First

Unsafe behavior around tools, materials, vehicles, or people may result in being asked to stop work or leave for the day.

Everyone shares responsibility for maintaining a safe environment.

---

### 6. We Practice Governance Through Real Work

We are experimenting with collaborative decision-making inspired by sociocracy.

This means:

* discussing real operational needs,
* listening carefully,
* sharing responsibility,
* and learning how to coordinate together.

We are learning by doing.

---

## This Is a Living Document

These agreements will evolve over time as the workshop grows and new people participate.

# Vision & Mission

## Vision

We envision a local workshop ecosystem where people of different ages and backgrounds can:

* build useful things together,
* develop practical skills,
* support one another,
* and create pathways toward meaningful work and participation.

We believe strong communities grow through:

* shared effort,
* stewardship,
* trust,
* reciprocity,
* and practical collaboration.

---

## Mission

The mission of the workshop is to:

* create a shared space for building and learning,
* support hands-on participation,
* cultivate stewardship and responsibility,
* mentor through real projects,
* and slowly develop community infrastructure rooted in trust and reciprocity.

---

## Long-Term Possibilities

Over time, the workshop may support:

* youth apprenticeships,
* local fabrication,
* repair culture,
* cooperative projects,
* community resilience efforts,
* tool sharing,
* small business incubation,
* or seed funding for promising local initiatives.

But none of that is guaranteed.

We are focused first on building:

* consistency,
* trust,
* shared culture,
* and operational reality.

---

## What We Are Not

We are not:

* a school,
* a charity,
* a traditional nonprofit,
* a startup incubator,
* or a polished institution.

At least not today.

This project is intentionally experimental and grounded.

We are trying to build something real slowly enough that it can actually sustain itself.

# Expectations

## What You Can Expect From This Space

We aim to create an environment that is:

* welcoming,
* practical,
* collaborative,
* experimental,
* and grounded in real work.

People here may be:

* building projects,
* repairing tools,
* organizing materials,
* gardening,
* fabricating,
* learning new skills,
* teaching others,
* or simply helping maintain the space.

---

## What Is Expected of Participants

You do not need to be an expert.

You do need to:

* show respect,
* contribute where you can,
* communicate honestly,
* take responsibility for your actions,
* and help care for the environment.

---

## Beginner Friendly

This is intended to be a baby-step environment.

You are allowed to:

* not know things,
* ask questions,
* make mistakes,
* learn slowly,
* and grow over time.

Curiosity and reliability matter more than experience.

---

## Reliability Matters

We value people who:

* follow through,
* return when they say they will,
* help without needing constant supervision,
* and contribute steadily over time.

Trust is built through repeated participation.

---

## Ownership and Projects

* develop their own projects,

* build products,
* or collaborate on shared ventures.
